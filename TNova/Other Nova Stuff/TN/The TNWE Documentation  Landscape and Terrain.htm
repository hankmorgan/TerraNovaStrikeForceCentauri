*** Landscape and Terrain ***





Getting started

After installation of TN and the editors all you have to do to get started is to select which map you like to edit. Depending on the TN-version you're using here is a description of how to get started:

  • TN full version: Here is a list of all the maps. All map-files have the name "MAP*.RES" and are located in the "MAPS"-directory on your CD or -if you installed them- on your HD.
    A quick example would be TNEDIT {name of your cddrive:}\MAPS\MAP3.RES

  • TN demo 1: There is only one map: "MAP50A.RES". It is located in the "DATA"-directory in your TN-directory.
    A quick example would be TNEDIT DATA\MAP50A.RES

  • TN demo 2: There are three maps: "MAP2D.RES, MAP3D.RES, MAP4D.RES". They are located in the "MAPS"-directory in your TN-directory.
    A quick example would be TNEDIT MAPS\MAP2D.RES



    After starting TNEDIT:

    You can press CTRL+H to display the help pages. Alternatively you can download the keys-commands as GIF(16KB) or as PS(14KB).

    This screen is an example what you usually get after startup. Try walking around with the cursor-movement keys (a,s,w,d)!

    This is an explanation of what is displayed in the statusbar:

    Heigth of tile under cursor Values between -2048 and 2047 are accepted. To change the evaluation press h.
    Note: All numbers except for the X and Y coordinates of the cursor position are displayed and entered as decimal numbers. The X and Y coordinates are displayed as hexadecimal numbers.
    Eye's heigth above ground for i-g-g-p Values between 0 and 1000 are accepted. To change the evaluation press shift-y.
    x-coordinate of tile under cursor Values between 0 and 0x1ff (dec. 511) are accepted. To move the cursor along the x-axis press a,d.
    y-coordinate of tile under cursor Values between 0 and 0x1ff (dec. 511) are accepted. To move the cursor along the y-axis press w,s.
    Note: Values for the x- and the y-coordinate are displayed hexadecimal!
    Type of the trees on tile under cursor Values between 0 and 6 are accepted. To change press x,shift-x,ctrl-x.
    Shadow of tile under cursor Values between 0 and 7 are accepted. To change the shadow press 1,2,3,4.
    Blast of tile under cursor Values are: b and X. To toggle press b.
    Blasted areas look like burned in the game.
    Complex Rendering of in-game-graphics-preview (i-g-g-p) Values are C and NOT-C. To toggle press shift-u.
    Complex rendering includes the sky and the trees. Normal rendering only renders the terrain.
    Precision of rendering in i-g-g-p Values are dark grey, grey and white. To cycle press ctrl-y.
    The lighter the better the perspective correction of near textures.
    Resolution of rendering of i-g-g-p Values between 1 and 4 are accepted. To cycle press ctrl-b.
    1,2,3 and 4 equal resolutions of 80x60, 160x120, 320x240 and 640x480 pixels. Complex rendering always renders in 640x480 pixels.
    Intensity of fog in i-g-g-p Values are n,f,h,w,i. n is the highest density, i is the lowest (no fog at all). To cycle press ctrl-f.
    Area-Mode Values are A and NOT-A To toggle press r.
    If in area mode all commands are applied to the marked area. Commands specified with (Sp) can only apply to Single Points. Commands specified as (Am) can only be applied to areas.
    Type of mapraytrace Values are R,T,D,H for Mapraytraced, Topview, Depthraytraced and Heigthraytraced. To cycle press ctrl-v.
    Scalemode of mapraytrace Values are M and NOT-M. To toggle press ctrl-o.
    If in scalemode, the evaluationmap will be stretched to the maximum.
    Type of displaymap Values are S,H,T for Shadow, Heigth and Trees. To cycle press ctrl-u.

    Some global information about terrain-handling in TN: There are a low resolution and a high resolution map describing the landscape in TN. The high resolution map is the one you actually walk on in the game. The low resolution map is only used when you look outside this inner area, so it's mostly used to display the mountains at the end of the world (namely: the horizon). Currently only the high resolution map can be edited in TNEdit. To edit the lowres map you have to use the map-export (shift-e) and the map-import (shift-i) functions and edit the exported heigthmap with an ordinary painting program.
    Each point in the map consists of four values: The heigth of the upper left corner in the range between -2048 and 2047. The texture the tile is covered with and the orientation of this texture (each texture can be rotated in steps of 90°). The amount of sunlight on that square (value range between 0 and 7). The blast bit: This determines whether the square is burned (like after a tree has burned, the earth under the tree is burned). This image should demonstrade the structure of the maps in TN:


    There is one additional attribute each square of 2x2 tiles: The tree type. This attribute determines whether and which trees "grow" on the tiles. There are 6 different types. The best way to understand the tree type is to use the x and shift-x keys to set the "Tree="-value displayed in the status bar to a value different from 0 and then to press the ctrl-u key to select "T" as the "Type of displaymap" (T means that trees are displayed). Now press x and shift-x and take a look what is changing. Note: You must be in normal zoomed mode (see below) and on a map located on planet 0 or 4 to be able to see any tree. Display of trees is not yet supported for planet 1,2 and 3! To learn what's about the planets, please read below!


    Press p to toggle zoomed and unzoomed display. Zoomed you see above and this is unzoomed (on a different map ;):


    Press f to toggle between fast and normal drawing mode in any zoom-mode.
    (Fast mode looks really ugly and is intended only for slow computers.)
    zoomed, fast zoomed, normal
    unzoomed, fast unzoomed, normal


    Press v to open the tile texture selection menu:

    Use a,s,w,d to select the tile, press space to choose it as new texture or v to abort the selection.
    Back in the mapeditor you can press space to set the texture again.


    What's about the Planets? Well, in the TN-Demos, all missions take place on an earth like planet, which numbers are 0 and 4. 0 is the planet during the day and 4 during night. Actually in the demo there is only the daylight-version of this planet available. In the full version of TN there are three more planets: Planet 1 is an icy cold planet, planet 2 is a sandy hot planet and planet 3 is a moon. If you specify a map to be loaded into TNEDIT, automatically the planet specified in the map-file will be loaded. Nevertheless, you can specify an alternative planet on the command line. If you save the map, the planet will be changed in the map-file to the planet you've specified. Be careful: If you change a planet in a map-file, then you have to change the planet in the miss-file that describes the map. See the EdMiss-Section for a description of the miss-file!


    Press j to toggle the display of the sideview evaluation lines and the normal mapedit mode.

    Sideview allows you to see the terrain your editing from the side thus allowing you to get a better understanding of the evaluation of the terrain.
    With shift-b you can blur the heigth. This means that the evaluation will get smoother and less bumpy.
    With h you can set the heigth and with n you can set the current heigth again without having to type in the numbers again.
